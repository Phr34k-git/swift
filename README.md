# Swift

Since many people have been asking about the situation with **Misery**, here is a clear explanation.

Misery removed **Rando** from the partnership, claiming that **XTernal code** was being used inside Swift. However, XTernal was released under the **Apache 2.0 license**, which explicitly allows users to use, modify, redistribute, and even commercially use the code as long as the license terms are followed. Because of this, the claim that “you cannot use the code” does not align with how the Apache 2.0 license works.

Another concern for us was that old **FischGPT code** was later shared as “proof,” along with statements about potentially adding **grabbers or other malicious code**. Due to the ongoing drama and uncertainty around the situation, we do not feel comfortable continuing to offer paid access to something like this.

Because of this, we have decided to make **Swift publicly available for everyone** instead.
